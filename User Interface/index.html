Palo Alto EV Charger Analysis

About

This project focuses on the placement of Electric Vehicle Charging Stations (EVCS) in the Palo Alto region. The goal is to analyze the existing charging infrastructure and forecast the future demand for charging points in the area.

The map below visualizes the existing charging stations (marked in green) and the proposed new charging stations (marked in blue) in the Palo Alto region. By entering a specific zip code, you can explore the charging infrastructure in that particular area.

The data used in this project includes:

  • Existing charging stations
  • Proposed new charging stations
  • Forecasted charging points for 2024 and 2025

By analyzing this data, we aim to provide insights into the current state of the charging infrastructure and help plan for future expansion to meet the growing demand for electric vehicle charging in the Palo Alto region.

Findings:

To tackle the pressing issues of energy sustainability and environmental impact, the adoption of electric vehicles has emerged as a viable solution.However, the growth of the EV market is frequently constrained by insufficient charging infrastructure. This paper provides a data-driven analysis of existing EV charging facilities to strategically plan future expansions. The research employs three distinct analytical approaches: predicting the necessary increase in charging station ports using the BIRCH clustering algorithm with a Silhouette Score of 0.52, forecasting the energy consumption of EV charging stations using an XGBoost regression model achieving an R-squared value of 0.81,and determining optimal locations for new charging stations using a Decision Tree algorithm with an R-squared of 0.94.These methods offer comprehensive support for urban planning and environmental sustainability initiatives,enabling informed decision-making to facilitate the integration of EVs into the urban landscape and promote environmental sustainability.
